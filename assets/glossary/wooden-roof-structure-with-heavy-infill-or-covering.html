Wooden roof structure supporting a heavy flat or domed roof

Wooden beams or trusses and joists, supporting heavy roof infill. Examples include a wooden roof with a layer of earth covered by clay roof tiles, found in high precipitation regions around the world. Another example of heavy covering is stone tiles, which are used for roofing.

Heavy roof infills are made from mixture of clay, straw and tamped earth. New layers are added annually to the roof as a protection against rain and snow percolation. These are predominantly single-storey buildings and are found in rural areas of Eastern Turkey, Iran, Afghanistan, Pakistan, India, China, as well as in the Andean highlands and other regions.
